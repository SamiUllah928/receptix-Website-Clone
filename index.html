Why Fraser Health?

Fraser Health (FH) is responsible for the delivery of hospital and community-based health services to over 1.9 million people in 20 diverse communities from Burnaby to Fraser Canyon on the traditional territories of the Coast Salish and Nlaka’pamux Nations. The Fraser region is home to 32 First Nations communities and 6 Métis Chartered communities. Our team of nearly 45,000 staff, medical staff and volunteers is dedicated to serving our patients, families and communities to deliver on our vision : better health, best in health care. The Executive Director of Maternity, Infant, Child and Youth (MICY) is seeking a result-oriented and agile professional with healthcare leadership experience for the Temporary Full Time Director of Clinical Operations role within the MICY portfolio until August 30, 2024. Our ideal candidate is passionate about achieving the best possible care for patients while improving their quality of life. The Director is a clear, caring and courageous leader that brings a strategic approach to providing and implementing services across the portfolio. As the Director of MICY, you will be an advocate for continuous improvement and bring a positive approach to developing a resilient team while managing multi-site responsibilities.

The Director is accountable for :

Spearheading the strategic and operational planning, integration, implementation and delivery of MICY across the organization. Collaborating with key internal and external partners to communicate needs and outcomes while operating within the approved budgets and financial plans. Implementing the standardization of practice, quality, safety and cost effectiveness support by creating integrated person-centered care. Leading through effective feedback, retention strategies and work practices to support safety, engagement, respect and well-being for all parties. Acting as the liaison supporting the shared strategic and operational priorities of the Foundation and Fraser Health.

The successful candidate will have :

A Master's degree in a field related to healthcare management; and Progressively senior leadership experience in a large complex, multi-site organization; or An equivalent combination of education, training, and experience.
